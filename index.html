Why is the Rhogam (Anti-D) Injection Crucial?

If a mother has a Rhesus Negative (Rh-) blood type and her baby has a Rhesus Positive (Rh+) blood type, the mother's body may produce antibodies that attack the baby's red blood cells. This is known as Rh incompatibility.

The Rhogam (Anti-D) injection prevents the mother's immune system from developing these dangerous antibodies. It is typically administered during pregnancy (around 28 weeks) and within 72 hours after delivery, miscarriage, or abdominal trauma.

Time is critical. Getting the injection within the 72-hour window postpartum is vital for the safety of future pregnancies.

Quick Facts

  • Required if Mother is Rh- and Father is Rh+.

  • Needs storage in a cold chain (refrigerated between 2°C and 8°C).

  • Must be prescribed and administered by a qualified healthcare professional.
